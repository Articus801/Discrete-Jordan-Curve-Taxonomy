ABSTRACT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This computational research folder presents a fully automated, data-driven framework
for validating and exploring Toeplitz's conjecture (the Inscribed Square Problem)
across diverse topological families in R². While traditional analytical methods break
down when encountering non-differentiable fractal perimeters or rigid polygonal corners,
this project demonstrates a robust discrete solution framework.

We introduce the Central Chord Radial Transform (CCRT)—a coordinate-free algorithm
that transforms boundary coordinate distributions into unoriented internal chord arrays
("straw clouds"). By tracking the behavior of these arrays under varied resolutions,
spatial translations, and geometric deformations, we expose the deep structural mechanics
of configuration spaces.

CORE METRIC DISCOVERIES & EMPIRICAL RESULTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. TOPOLOGICAL POTENTIAL WELLS & GRADIENT CONVERGENCE
   - Discoveries: Mapping the total variance of the internal midpoint cloud across a
     spatial translation grid reveals a strictly convex, parabolic potential well.
     The absolute global minimum of this well isolates a unique structural center of
     mass symmetry for any closed loop.
   - Engineering Application: We leveraged this gradient to construct an Autonomous
     Self-Centering engine driven by numerical gradient descent. Given an arbitrary,
     heavily offset boundary, the tracking loop automatically slides its origin
     coordinates to achieve absolute alignment with the true symmetry axis.

2. RESOLUTION CONVERGENCE & THE NUMERICAL TRAP
   - Discoveries: Sweeping identical custom geometries across progressively denser
     sampling resolutions (N = 100 to 1200) exposes a fundamental discretization threshold.
     Coarse grids introduce high informational noise (yielding false-positive loops),
     while ultra-dense grids introduce numerical starvation where infinitesimal coordinate
     gaps mask true intersections. A stable mathematical signature plateaus elegantly at
     the N=600 node boundary.

3. CAUSTIC ENVELOPES AS SPECIFIC TOPOLOGICAL FINGERPRINTS
   - Discoveries: Midpoint caustics serve as structural fingerprints defining unique
     equivalence classes of shapes:
     * Perfect Circles: Collapse completely into a point singularity at (0,0).
     * Bumpy Bezier Loops: Open up dynamically into organic, multi-pronged star configurations.
     * Koch Snowflakes: Fracture into self-similar miniature fractal clouds at the barycenter.
     * Sharp Polygons: Force rigid, linear triskelions or stiff geometric rings.

4. NON-CONVEXITY & TOROIDAL MANIFOLD PROJECTIONS
   - Discoveries: Subjecting non-convex geometries (L-shapes) to the CCRT engine reveals
     that the chord configuration space physically detaches from the shape interior.
     Midpoint structures project "ghost manifolds" directly into empty space outside the
     boundary walls.
   - Chaikin Smoothing: Integrating Chaikin corner-cutting onto non-convex shapes transforms
     rigid T-junction intersections into fluid, sweeping curves. This empirically confirms
     the user's hypothesis of "toroidal influence," visually displaying the flat 2D
     projection of a smooth, immersed higher-dimensional Möbius manifold.

5. CONCENTRIC MULTIPLICITY & SELF-SIMILAR GEOMETRIC RESONANCE
   - Discoveries: High-frequency periodic boundaries (12-Peak Starburst Waves) introduce
     distinct concentric scalar levels. Square configurations duplicate internally, creating
     nested, scaled clones that align simultaneously with outer wave peaks and inner
     wave valleys. Rotational symmetries act as generators, forcing interlaced families of
     inscribed objects to emerge through discrete angular reflections.
